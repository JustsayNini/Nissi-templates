February 18th: Gambian Independence Day

What happened on the 18th of February?

So the Gambia is this tiny, skinny sliver of a roommate living inside Senegal's house, refusing to move out. Britain was its landlord...until it wasn't.

On February 18, 1965, the Gambians basically looked at the UK and said, "Yeah so..thanks for the tea, but we prefer benachin." They pulled off the chillest breakup in the history of nonchalant breakups, led by Dawda Jawara, and became a sovereign nation without the usual protests and guns and explosions and death and end of the world.

Eventually, they decided being a monarchy was cringe and went full Republic in 1970. Its the ultimate "size doesn't matter" success story of West Africa. And you can learn more about it... if you can find the link :3.
