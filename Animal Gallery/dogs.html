PET-ty Dogs Gallery

The Golden Retriever

This is sunshine with a fur coat. The Golden Retriever is probably the happiest dog alive - they always seem like they are smiling, even when they want to rip your head off (which is unlikely). They are patient, ultra-friendly, and will do anything for you to praise them... yes, they are literally people pleasers. They are also super clingy - they follow you around like they're afraid you'll collapse and die at any moment. If the way they worry about their owners all the time wasn't so adorable, I would be insulted. We're not that fragile.

Even though their common sense is questionable sometimes, they are very intelligent and are therefore easier to be taught to "roll-over".

FUN FACT: They have "soft-mouths", so they can hold an egg in their mouths without breaking it. Golden Retrievers are the perfect choice for anyone lonely-- I mean, seeking companionship. You can learn more about themHERE... though I suppose I should warn you; they are terrible at being guard dogs.

The Rottweiler

Looks like it could kill you.
Could kill you.
But is also secretly a cinnamon roll :3.

Rottweilers are built like tanks. You'll always see one involved in military or police operations. They are very loyal and very good at defense so you will feel very safe with them around.

As scary as they look, they are really soft when it comes to their owners. They believe that they are light enough for yoy to carry them, which explains why they always try to cut off blood circulation to your legs by pretending they're a lap dog. FUN FACT: They were almost extinct in the 1800s, but being such good guard dogs bascially saved them!

The Chihuahua

Chihuahuas are basically "karens" in canine form. They are spicier than the shawarmas I keep telling myself I can finish in under 5 minutes.
Chihuahuas are these tiny gremlins that've managed to convince the wor that they are dogs. They have short tempers (which compliments their size). Interestingly, they have a shocking brain to body ratio and are "big-brained". I find them quite dim-witted though.

Apparently, they used to be worshipped by the Aztecs. This is why they think they rule your yard. They have a spot spot on their heads called a "molera" like human babies, which also closes up as they get older. They live for 20 years max. If you choose them as a pet, you'll be with them for a while.

The Shiba Inu

I am half-convinced the Shiba Inu is a cat disguised as a dog. Maybe they are cousins.
The Shiba Inu is native to Japan and acts accordingly. This dg will make you question you were ever relevant in anyone's (or thing's) life. They like to keep to themselves clean by grooming like cats (which is why I am almost certain that they are cats) and will judge you if you are the type of person to keep your space messy. With cleanliness comes logic and smarts, which you will absolutely find in this breed. Shiba Inus are incrediby intelligent and observant. So if you are upset because your Shiba Inu doesn't understand when you call it's name, it actually does understand. It's just ignoring you. You talk too much.

The German Shepard

The German Shepard is the one dog you should be certain is better than you.

They are common in the police and military sector, and are equally fierce in terms of protection and loyalty as house pets. Like the Rottweiler, they are soft for their owners, and will kill anyone that dares point a gun at them without thinking twice. Another dog to consider if you want to feel like you're walking next to a military tank on the way to the dog park.
